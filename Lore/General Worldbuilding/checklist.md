# Checklist for worldbuilding your fantasy world

** Family dynamics **
- patriarchal / matriarchal / egalitarian?
- nuclear families / extended families / communal living?
- who raises the children?

** Level of Development **
- tehcnology
- medicine
- science
- infrastructure
- art
- variation between regions/nations

** Langueage **
- nomenclature
- how born
- words without translation
- how culture shapes language
- how places are named
- nicknames? surnames?
- multiple languages/dialects?
- spoken vs written language

** Looks and Clothing **
- colors, shapes, styles, materials
- differences between classes, professions, regions, genders
- tattoos, piercings, body modifications
- hairstyles, makeup
- standards of beauty

** Music **
- instruments
- styles/genres
- role in society (entertainment, ritual, communication)
- famous musicians/composers
- music education

** Spare Time **
- hobbies, things done for fun
- sports, games, art, entertainment
- how guided by values
- preservation, sharing
- typical themes, stories, lessons
- vices

** History **
- major events, turning points
- wars, putsches, revolutions, tragedies
- natural disasters, famines, plagues
- influential figures, heroes, villains
- cultural shifts, renaissances
- how history recorded, preserved, taught

** Religion and Spirituality **
- effect on laws, holidays, daily life, society
- rituals, ceremonies, festivals
- places of worship
- Gods, deities, pantheons
- religious leaders, institutions
- religious texts, myths, legends

** Traditions and Customs **
- rites of passage (birth, adulthood, marriage, death)
- holidays, festivals
- social etiquette, manners
- gift-giving, hospitality
- food and drink customs
- marriage, dating, relationships
- symbols
- values
- taboos

** Tools **
- common tools for daily life, work, art
- specialized tools for professions, crafts
- materials used to make tools
- technological level of tools
- how tools shape society, culture
- weapons

** Buildings **
- architectural styles
- materials used
- types of buildings (homes, public buildings, religious buildings)
- layout of cities, towns, villages
- how buildings reflect culture, values

** Education **
- schools, universities, etc.
- subjects taught
- methods of teaching
- who has access to education
- role of education in society
- mode of teaching (oral, written, practical, tutoring)

** Food and Drink **
- staple foods, ingredients
- cooking methods, utensils
- meals and eating customs
- beverages (alcoholic and non-alcoholic)
- ways produced (farming, hunting, gathering)
- food preservation methods
- special dishes for holidays, celebrations

** Water **
- sources of water (rivers, lakes, wells, aqueducts)
- methods of water purification
- water storage and distribution systems
- cultural significance of water (rituals, symbolism)
- access to clean water (who has it, who doesn't)
- sewers and waste management
- irrigation for agriculture

** Politics and Government **
- relationship between other cultures/nations
- types of government (monarchy, democracy, oligarchy, theocracy, etc.)
- political structure (branches of government, roles of officials)
- laws and legal system
- political parties, factions
- what is prohibited by law

** Economy **
- import, export
- currency, trade systems
- livelyhoods, professions

** Societal Troubles **
- poverty, crime, corruption
- discrimination, inequality
- war, conflict
- environmental issues
- disease, health crises
